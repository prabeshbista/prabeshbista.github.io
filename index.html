About me

My Story

Hello there! I'm Prabesh Bista, hailing from the breathtaking landscapes of Nepal, and I'm thrilled to have you on my website. Growing up in the vibrant city of Kathmandu, I've embarked on an exciting academic journey that has taken me far from home and deep into the world of scientific exploration.

My educational journey began at Tri-Chandra Multiple Campus, where I cultivated the foundations of my passion for science. Upon completing my undergraduate degree, I took a bold step and headed to Taiwan for an internship. That experience ignited a fascination for hands-on research, leading me to work as a Research Assistant under the guidance of Dr. Cheng-Tien Chiang for a year. Now, I am a PhD student in Dr. Chiang’s lab., focusing on electron interactions within solid matter. My focus revolves around employing laser-based photoelectron spectroscopy to unlock the mysteries of these interactions. Through meticulous experimentation, I'm designing and constructing experiments, all while meticulously analyzing the resulting data.

But there's more to me than lab work! When I'm not busy experimenting, you'll find me engrossed in news updates, diving into non-fiction books, or exploring the outdoors through hiking, football, and cycling. I'm a firm believer in embracing life's various facets, so I also indulge in documentaries, movies, and even cooking – where creativity knows no bounds.
